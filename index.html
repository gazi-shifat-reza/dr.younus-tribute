Dr. Muhammad Yunus

The pioneer the concepts of Microcredit and Microfinance.

Here is the timeline of Dr. Muhammad Yunus's life:

  • 1940 - Born in chattogram, Bangladesh.
  • 1957 - He enrolled in the Department of Economics at Dhaka University.
  • 1960 - He completed his BA.
  • 1961 - He completed his MA and Later, he was appointed lecturer in economics in Chittagong College.
  • 1965 - He received a Fulbright scholarship to study in the United States and he obtained his PhD in economics from the Vanderbilt University Graduate Program in Economic Development (GPED).
  • 1971 - During the Bangladesh Liberation War, Yunus founded a citizen's committee and ran the Bangladesh Information Center, with other Bangladeshis in the United States, to raise support for liberation. He also published the Bangladesh Newsletter from his home in Nashville.
  • 1975 - After observing the famine of 1974, he became involved in poverty reduction and established a rural economic programme as a research project. He developed a Nabajug (New Era) Tebhaga Khamar (three share farm) which the government adopted as the Packaged Input Programme.
  • 1976 - He given the small loans to the poor family, they repayed the money and hence microcredit was a viable business model. Yunus lent US$27 of his money to 42 women in the village, who made a profit of BDT 0.50 (US$0.02) each on the loan. Thus, Yunus is credited with the idea of microcredit.
  • 1989 - He started Grameen Motsho and Grameen Krishi projects.
  • 2001 - For his work with Grameen, Yunus was named an Ashoka: Innovators for the Public Global Academy Member.
  • 2006- Yunus was awarded the Nobel Peace prize, along with Grameen Bank, for their efforts to create economic and social development.
  • 2008- Texas declared 14 January as "Muhammad Yunus Day". In the same year he was rated number 2 in Foreign Policy magazine's list of the 'Top 100 Global Thinkers'.
  • 2012- Yunus was named by Fortune Magazine in March 2012 as one of 12 greatest entrepreneurs of the current era.

    • Beside, He is one of only seven persons to have won the Nobel Peace Prize, Presidential Medal of Freedom, and the Congressional Gold Medal. Other notable awards include the Ramon Magsaysay Award in 1984, the World Food Prize, the International Simon Bolivar Prize (1996), the Prince of Asturias Award for Concord and the Sydney Peace Prize in 1998, and the Seoul Peace Prize in 2006.
